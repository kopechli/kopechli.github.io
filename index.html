The Algorithms

In 6th grade i was saving money from a zero allowance! To buy popular science, Focus or PC Magazine. At that point, i had a broken keyboard i found in a dumpster and i was pretending, typing on it. Those days, children were sent to computer schools, to get a diploma in computers. We were poor so my older brother was the one that got to go to computer school.

I learned about operating systems and Pascal from his notes. I was a dyslexic kid that gaved away his right to oral exams to compete in equal plane with the other students. By high school no one knew and no one can tell that i was dyslexic. I didn’t need therapy or doctors, because i treated it like handicap and a head start for the others, i always played in hard mode. I got a card from my local area library and started reading like no one else. I probably read the entire library by the end of the high school. I was kenny, no matter what, i am coming back! That is the mentality developed in me, at that time.

My journey in the software development world started in high school when i was introduced to algorithms. I excelled in computers and communication technology classes, although i did not had a PC at home. And when i got one(after working at a factory), i disappeared. After the relentless, gaming like theres no tomorrow(Diablo II) phase pased, i started breaking and fixing the operating system and subsequently learning and i was buying 56 kbps dial up internet line when i had the money to explore the internet.

Work Market

I am ready to start , that is what i was thinking! But according to others i wasn't. With in an underdeveloped industry with no work experience in the sector, no connections and with nobody willing to hire a greenhorn. I ended up working as ICT help desk agent. I remembered my professor's word of advice,one of those days, "Get the f.. out of here if you can!" I go from agent to floor assistant to back-office in 7 months and all that with the same pay! Oh and i was making visits to other departments as pc technician to!

I finished there and i spent a year trying to find a job! So, i decided i will work for the army instead of doing a compulsory service! With my background i can get in easy! I thought. They told me that the signals and communication is very difficult position to get in. There was only 8 position for the entire country, if i am not from a military or political family, i do not have a chance and that i need to pass the physicals and the athletic exams first. I am Kenny!

It ain't over till it's over

So... i am discharged from the army as a signals and communications second lieutenant and i am full of confidence (military can do that to you). I am contemplating my next steps, i familiarize myself with the new technologies in web development, for a moment it felt like, it was right! BUT... i am after all Kenny. My father diagnosed with C(a bad one),my grandpa need of constant monitoring and and visits to doctor after a life threatening surgery he had, my mother suffered a nervous breakdown and i ended up collecting the pieces.

Between ERs, chemotherapy session, psychiatric hospitals, doctor visitation and microbiology laboratories, let's say my life was static. In 2018 my father's suffering ended, i managed to fix most of the pieces and by the end of the year i was working at a car wash! But i was happy , because i started working again. In 2019 i managed to re enter the web development sector and in 2020 i am a collateral damage. I am Kenny!
